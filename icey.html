Ranges reflect Denebola scored (−5) or not scored (0). Denebola at sidereal ♌ 27°44′ aligns in ecliptic longitude with the cazimi cluster (Sun 0°29′, Mars 0°47′, Moon 0°59′) but sits ~12° north in ecliptic latitude — not a physical conjunction. In a Placidus framework that privileges real spatial geometry, the longitude-only projection is inconsistent with the house system's philosophy. Both options are preserved for the astrologer's judgment.

The chart has two major interpretive variables: the Moon's cazimi/combust status and the Denebola question. Together they create a range for the Moon from 0 (cazimi, no Denebola — a Moon that breaks even) to −16 (combust with Denebola — a Moon nearly as debilitated as Jupiter). The Sun and Mars shift by 5 points depending on the Denebola decision, which affects whether Mars is dominant by a commanding margin (+19) or a strong one (+14), and whether the Sun competes with Saturn for second place (+11) or ties it (+6).

Mars leads the chart in all scenarios — cazimi, fast (0°39'/day, 126% of mean), direct, conjunct Jupiter, with term and face in Leo. At +19 without Denebola, Mars is dominant by a wide margin. At +14 with Denebola, Mars still leads decisively. As lord of the Ascendant in a night chart, Mars is both the native's primary instrument and the planet most likely to cause harm through its own excess.

The Sun ranges from +11 (without Denebola) to +6 (with). At +11, the Sun is a genuinely strong planet — domicile dignity (+5) with meaningful accidental support (+6), second only to Mars. At +6, the Sun ties Saturn, its strength more essential than accidental. Either way, the Sun is the most qualitatively competent planet in the chart.

Saturn (+6) is unchanged by Denebola. Its strength is entirely accidental (+16) — free from the Sun, oriental, fast (0°05'/day, ~2.5x mean), direct, sextile Jupiter — built on top of essential ruin (detriment and peregrine, −10). Saturn acts constantly but performs its own nature poorly.

Venus (+4) is the chart's emotional anchor — angular in H4, free from the Sun, in full hayz — but slower than her mean (1°06' vs. 1°12') and void of course. She delivers warmth and domestic stability but with limited follow-through.

Jupiter (−17) remains the weakest planet in all scenarios. In detriment, peregrine, combust, conjunct Mars, occidental, and contrary to hayz. The fast speed (+2) is the only bright spot.

Notes on methodology: Mars is scored as cazimi (0°18' from the Sun, at the traditional 0°17' boundary — treated as cazimi given the phenomenological reality of complete solar absorption). Moon at 0°30' is outside the strict cazimi orb; both cazimi and combust interpretations are presented. Denebola is dual-scored: ecliptic longitude alignment is within standard 1° orb, but ~12° ecliptic latitude separation means no physical conjunction exists in the observable sky (confirmed via Stellarium). In a Placidus framework, spatial geometry takes precedence over projection. Both options are shown for transparency.
