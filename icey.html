The Moon's score depends entirely on whether the 0°30′ separation is read as cazimi or combust. At cazimi (+5), the Moon scores −5 overall — weak but not catastrophic, held afloat by the Jupiter conjunction, increasing light, and occidental advantage. At combust (−6), the Moon collapses to −16, making it nearly as debilitated as Jupiter and second-weakest in the chart. The 11-point swing between these two readings is the single largest interpretive variable in the entire table. The difference is not academic: it determines whether the native's emotional life is empowered-but-constrained or genuinely impaired.

Mars (+14) leads the chart decisively — cazimi, fast (0°39′/day, 126% of mean), direct, conjunct Jupiter, with term and face in Leo. Denebola (−5) cuts into the total but Mars's diverse accidental strengths absorb the blow. As lord of the Ascendant in a night chart, Mars is both the native's primary instrument and the planet most likely to cause harm through its own excess.

The Sun (+6) and Saturn (+6) tie for second from opposite foundations. The Sun's strength is almost entirely essential — domicile in Leo (+5) with thin accidental support (+1), as the benefic and malefic conjunctions cancel and Denebola erodes the sign joy. Saturn's strength is entirely accidental (+16) — free from the Sun, oriental, fast (0°05′/day, ~2.5× mean), direct, sextile Jupiter — built on top of essential ruin (detriment and peregrine, −10). The Sun knows what it is but can't always act; Saturn acts constantly but doesn't know what it's doing.

Venus (+4) is the chart's emotional anchor — angular in H4, free from the Sun, in full hayz — but slower than her mean (1°06′ vs. 1°12′) and void of course. She delivers warmth and domestic stability but with limited follow-through. As ruler of the 7th, partnerships begin with genuine feeling but may stall before completion.

Jupiter (−17) remains the weakest planet. In detriment, peregrine, combust, conjunct Mars, occidental, and contrary to hayz. The fast speed (+2) is the only bright spot, and it merely means Jupiter rushes ineffectively through a sign that rejects it.
