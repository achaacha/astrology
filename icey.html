Interpretive Summary

1. ♂ Mars  +12  (ess. +3 · acc. +9)
2. ☉ Sun    +6  (ess. +5 · acc. +1)
3. ♀ Venus  +6  (ess. −5 · acc. +11)
4. ♄ Saturn +4   (ess. −10 · acc. +14)
5. ☽ Moon   −3  (ess. −5 · acc. +2)
6. ☿ Mercury −5  (ess. −5 · acc. 0)
7. ♃ Jupiter −19 (ess. −10 · acc. −9)

Denebola dominates the accidental picture. The malefic fixed star at sidereal Leo 27°46′ falls within 1° of the Sun, Moon, and Mars — the chart's entire cazimi cluster. Each receives −5, which is the single largest accidental debility after combustion. This substantially reshapes the hierarchy: Mars remains the strongest planet but drops from commanding to moderate, the Sun falls into a tie with Venus, and the Moon crosses into negative territory.

Mars (+12) is still the strongest planet in the chart, but the margin is thinner. It carries positive essential dignity (term and face in Leo, +3) and strong accidental advantages: cazimi, direct, conjunct Jupiter. Denebola cuts into that lead (−5), but Mars's diverse sources of accidental strength absorb the blow. The Denebola conjunction is interpretively significant, however — it colours the cazimi with themes of misfortune through impulsiveness or nobility misdirected.

The Sun (+6) is the only planet in full domicile, with the highest essential quality in the chart (+5). Its accidental score is now barely positive (+1): the benefic/malefic conjunction cancellation (Jupiter +5, Mars −5) plus sign joy (+3) are offset by Denebola (−5). The Sun's strength is almost entirely essential — it knows what it is and can deliver on its nature, but the circumstances conspire to limit its reach.

Venus (+6) ties the Sun on a completely opposite footing: essentially peregrine (−5) but accidentally robust (+11). Angularity in H4, freedom from the Sun, full hayz, and direct motion build her accidental score, though void of course limits follow-through. Venus is conspicuous and active but lacks essential grounding — a person of visible influence who doesn't quite know what to do with it.

Saturn (+4) presents the starkest essential/accidental contrast. In detriment and peregrine (−10 essential) but scoring +14 accidentally through freedom from the Sun, oriental position, direct motion, and a sextile from Jupiter. This is an incompetent figure in a position of some influence — active and visible but delivering Saturnine themes in a distorted Cancerian register.

The Moon (−3) now finishes negative despite cazimi. Peregrine (−5), void of course (−2), at the end of Leo (−2), and conjunct Denebola (−5) overwhelm the cazimi (+5), Jupiter conjunction (+5), increasing light (+1), and occidental advantage (+2). The Moon is the most conflicted planet in the chart — intensely activated by cazimi but undermined at every other turn.

Mercury (−5) is under the beams at 16°34′ from the Sun, oriental when it would prefer to be occidental, and peregrine. Direct motion (+4) barely offsets the penalties. Mercury is the most neutral planet — not strong enough to matter, not weak enough to be a clear problem.

Jupiter (−19) is the weakest planet by a wide margin. In detriment in Virgo and peregrine (−10 essential), then combust (−6), conjunct malefic Mars (−5), occidental (−2), and contrary to hayz (−2). As a natural benefic, this severely limits the chart's access to Jupiterian goods. Whatever Jupiter signifies by house rulership will be delivered with difficulty and distortion.

Notes on methodology: Moon and Mars are scored as cazimi per instruction, though Mars at 0°18′ from the Sun falls just outside the strict 0°17′ boundary — an interpretive judgment that recognises the effective unity of the triple conjunction. Velocity could not be scored without precise ephemeris daily-motion figures for 14 Sep 2004 and is marked 0 across the board; if daily-motion data are provided, each planet could shift ±2. Lahiri ayanamsa at epoch ≈ 23°54′. Fixed star positions are from the Orphic Astrology catalogue (Lahiri ayanamsa). Denebola at sidereal Leo 27°46′ is the only 1st/2nd magnitude star from the standard benefic/malefic lists within 1° of any planet. Procyon (sidereal Cancer 1°56′) falls ~1°07′ from Saturn but is not on the standard scoring list. Praesepe (sidereal Cancer 13°21′) is near Venus but is a nebular cluster, not a discrete 1st/2nd magnitude star.
