Interpretive Summary

Ranking by grand total:

  • 1. ♃ Jupiter +11
  • 2. ☉ Sun +10
  • 3. ♄ Saturn +9
  • 4. ☽ Moon +5
  • 5. ♂ Mars +1
  • 6. ☿ Mercury −13
  • 7. ♀ Venus −20

Jupiter emerges as the chart's strongest planet despite occupying the cadent 8th house and falling under the Sun's beams. Its strength is almost entirely accidental: direct and extraordinarily fast motion (261% of mean), oriental, in full hayz, and tightly conjunct Venus. Its essential dignity is modest — only term — which creates an interpretive tension: Jupiter acts powerfully and visibly, but its promise is limited to the minor dignity of term. A capable operator with only modest credentials.

The Sun is the only planet with substantial essential dignity (+8 from domicile and triplicity), anchoring its authority in genuine quality. Its accidental score is modest (+2), dragged down by the 8th house. This is a competent figure in a difficult circumstance — the Sun's quality is impeccable, but the 8th house limits its visibility and sphere of action.

Saturn holds domicile dignity (+5) and sits on the Ascendant — the most prominent accidental position. However, retrograde motion and contrariety to hayz erode the accidental gains. Saturn is essentially sound and accidentally placed to be noticed, but hampered in execution.

The Moon is the chart's most interesting case of the essential–accidental split. Peregrine (−5 essential), the Moon has no capacity to fulfill its nature well — it makes promises it cannot keep. Yet its accidental score is the second-highest in the chart (+10): angular in the 4th, free from the Sun, fast, waxing. This is the "incompetent person in a leadership role" archetype. The Moon can do a great deal; whether what it does is any good is another question.

Venus is the chart's weakest planet by a devastating margin (−20). Peregrine, retrograde, under the beams, slower than average, contrary to hayz, and now placed in the 8th house — every possible debilitating condition is present. The earlier 7th-house reading offered at least a thin life raft of angularity; with the cusp correction, that raft is gone and replaced by the −4 of a malefic house. Venus is accumulating debilities from both the essential and accidental columns with nothing to offset them.

Mercury and Mars both carry the peregrine penalty but diverge in opposite directions. Mars gains from being free of the Sun, direct, and fast (+6 accidental), keeping its total barely positive. Mercury (−13), even with the remarkable +10 from dual benefic conjunctions, is overwhelmed: retrograde, nearly stationary, under the beams, and now scored in the 8th house rather than the 7th. The benefic conjunctions with Venus and Jupiter are Mercury's only source of accidental strength — without them, Mercury would score −23. That the two benefics are themselves compromised (Venus catastrophically so) raises the question of how much their support is actually worth.

Four of seven planets are peregrine. Five of seven occupy the 8th house. The chart's functional strength concentrates in the Sun–Jupiter–Saturn triad, with Jupiter doing the most but promising the least, and the Sun promising the most but constrained by house. The Moon amplifies everything it touches without essential discrimination. The bottom of the chart is stark: Venus at −20 and Mercury at −13 are deeply compromised planets whose benefic conjunctions with each other and with Jupiter form a mutual support network among the weakened — a cluster of debilitated planets propping each other up inside a malefic house.
